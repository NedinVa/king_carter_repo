Letter from Robert Carter to William Cage, Esq., October 6, 1724

     Robert Carter writes to the trustee of the Fairfax estate, William Cage, Esq., in County Kent, England, October 6, 1724, to confirm that he has ordered merchant Micajah Perry to conclude a lease for the Virginia agency of the Northern Neck proprietary for seven more years.


Letter from Robert Carter to William Cage, Esq., October 6, 1724

-1 -

Rappa[hannock, Lancaster County, Virginia]   October. 6th. 1724

Sir:

     I have already writ to you in
full and have given Mr Perry instructions to conclude
with his Lordship & you upon a lease for your Estate
for seven years longer. I know the world is very censori=
ous & tis impossible for men under any tollerable
figure to live without enimies & there may be some perhaps
so busy to give you unagreable storys of my management
of your Estate, all the justice I Shall desire of you is tht
you will not receive any impressions to my prejudice
untill you allow me the opportunity to be heard for
my Self & I am not in the least doubt to demonstrat
tht every Step I have taken will redound to your interest
and those to whom the Estate will belong. pray My
humble respects to his Lordship I AM


Sr.
Your most obedient Humble Servt.

ROBERT CARTER

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Fairfax Papers, BR 227, folder 22. Huntington Library, Art Collections, and Botanical Gardens, San Marino, California. Printed: Wright. Letters of Robert Carter. . . . pp. 116-117.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to persons abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

This is the ecipient's copy; the conjoined address leaf bears the following: To | Wm: Cage Esqr | Of Milgate In the Parish of Bearshead | In the county of | Kent | Via Glasgow. There is no endorsement. The letter was signed by Carter in a very large and bold hand (indicated here by the use of bold italics) and sealed with his arms.

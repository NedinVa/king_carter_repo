Letter from Robert Carter to Captain Henry Morryson, August 5, 1727

     Robert Carter writes to Captain Henry Morryson, August 5, 1727, to summon him, "as one of the King's Collectors" and a judge of the court of Admiralty, to attend a trial of accused pirates from North Carolina.


Letter from Robert Carter to Captain Henry Morryson, August 5, 1727

-1-

Rappahannock [Corotoman Lancaster County, Virginia]

Augst. the 5th. 1727

Capt. Henry Morryson

Sir --

     I have appointed a Court of Admiralty
to be held at the Capitol in Williamsburg on the 15th: Instant for the
trial of some Certain pirates that were apprehended in Carolina and
by this time I reckon are brought to our publick Jail [sic] You as one
of the Kings Collectors are Constituted a Judge of this Court by the
Commission I do therefore hereby give you notice of the Said Court
and Time that you may give your Attendance Accordingly I am


Sir --
Yor: most humble Servt:

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter letter book, 1727 May-1728 July, Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia. There is a nineteenth-century copy of this letter in the Minor-Blackford Papers, James Monroe Law Office and Museum, Fredericksburg, Virginia.

The clerk automatically wrote "Rappa" at the head of the letter, but struck it oiut when the letter was to be sent to someone in the colony. The name of Carter's home, county, and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] Henry Morrison (Morryson) was a resident of Westmoreland County who was appointed a justice in 1732. (McIlwaine. Executive Journals of the Council. . . . , 4[1721-1739]:268. )

Letter from Robert Carter to John Burridge, July 9, 1728

     Robert Carter writes to Lyme Regis merchant John Burridge, July 9, 1728, to acknowledge a December 2, 1727, report of sales and two debts, and informing him of a bill of exchange and that he is not sending tobacco to him because the market in Lyme is so low.


Letter from Robert Carter to John Burridge, July 9, 1728

-1 -

Rappahannock, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

July the 9th: 1728

John Burridge Esqr.

Sir --

     I received your's of the 2d: of December with the Sales of my
10 hogsheads I am Sorry to find you talk of an Outstanding debt of £9:17:6
I hope this money wont be lost there was another outstanding Debt
that now you make no menton of. Your Market this year is so low
that I have not Sent any Tobacco to your Port, I have drawn a bill
of Exchange upon you for £16 payable to Mr. Charles Stagg which I desire
you to anwer at time and is all at present from


Sir
Your humble Servt:

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter letter book, 1727 May-1728 July, Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

Summary



Letter from Robert Carter to John Randolph, August 5, 1727

     Robert Carter writes to John Randolph, August 5, 1727, to direct the attorney general to act for the clerk of the Council, William Robertson, who had broken his leg, in handling the matters related to the trial of pirates in the Vice Admiralty Court.


Letter from Robert Carter to John Randolph, August 5, 1727

-1 -

Rappahannock, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

Augst: the 5th: 1727

John Randolph Esqr.

Sir --

     Yors: of the 3d: reachd me this morning at Rosegill Middle
sex Court I mett with the unhappy account of Poor William Robertsons
Deplorable disaster as he is desirous you Should act in his behalf
in this affair of the pyrates so am I too and do now Send you direct[ions]
to dispatch away proper Letters for Summoning the Judges Coll. Page
and Mr. Grimes will need none having already promised me to be
there from Middlesex Court I writ to Colonel Robinson and Shall give notice
Speedily to our Collr: and the Collr: of Patowmack however if you think
Summons necessary to these three Gent. absolutely necessary you
may do in it in respect to them as you think fitt. The Captains
of the men of Warr if any in place are in the Commission, If it
not be im proper in your Summons to let the Gent of the Council you to let may let them know
that the Day after the tryal I have appointed a Council, Altho I
beleive there will be little occasion for this I am with a great
deal of Sincerity


Sir --
Yor: most Faithfull humble Servt --

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter letter book, 1727 May-1728 July, Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia. There is a nineteenth-century copy of this letter in the Minor-Blackford Papers, James Monroe Law Office and Museum, Fredericksburg, Virginia.

Robert Carter generally did not use the return address "Rappahannock" (for the river on which he lived) for letters sent to persons within the colony, but the clerk has written it here. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] Robertson had broken his leg. See Carter's letter to Nathaniel Harrison 1727 August 7.

[2] William Robertson was clerk of the Council and apparently also of the Vice Admiralty Court in which the pirates' trial was to be held. Randolph was attorney general, and could readily substitute for Robertson.

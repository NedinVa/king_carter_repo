June 2, 1701
Letter from Robert Carter to Jonathan Mathews and John Goodwin, 1701 June 2

     Robert Carter writes the London merchants Jonathan Mathews and John Goodwin that he is one of the exectors of Ralph Wormeley and a guardian of Wormeley's sons; he is sending 27 hogsheads of tobaccoe belonging to the estate for sale. They shall have the majority of the business of the estate if he can arrange it. Carter reports the arrival of goods sent to Wormeley, and encloses an order for more to be charged to the estate.


Letter from Robert Carter to Jonathan Mathews and John Goodwin, June 2, 1701

-1-

[Rappahannock, Lancaster County, Virginia]

June the 2d. 1701

Jonathan Mathews &
John Goodwin --
Srs.

     Mr. Secretary Wormeley in his Will (a Coppy whereof is herein sent you)
hath Appointed meone of his Executrs. in the Minor Age of his Sons. In
Respect to Our Long Acquaintance, my Relation to his Widdow & Children
and the great Desire I ever had, and hope alwayes shallpreserve, to
befriend the fatherless, and not any Interest of my Own, Expecting nothing
but myLabour for my hire, and perhapps to be Censur'd for a Wrong
doer att Last I have Intermeddle withthat Estate & have Shipt the To=
-baccoe belonging thereto, 27 hogsheads whereof are on board [John] Burford & Consign'd
to you, they areof his own Cropps wch. you Comended very much last Year
I hope these may prove as good. I musttell you, 'tis the largest parcell Comes
to any One Merchant from that Concern, not at all to the likingof the
Rest, of the Exrs. tho' so long as you are kind in your Care of that Concern
I shallEndeavour to continue to you a Considerable part of the Consign=
-ments. your Goods to Esqr. Wormeley per Burford Came safe to hand, Except
the White sugr. wch. Cannot be heard of, Burford sayes, it never Came Aboard
pray Enquire into it, and sett the thing to Rights. In the Ballance ofyour
Last Accot. Currtt. you Debitt your self to that Estate £76:-:8 besides 21 hogsheads
unaccounted for, 15 of wch. your Lettrs. sayes, are sold, 6 Remaines unsold
of them you hope tomake Eleven pence per pound or more soe far in
answer to your Lettrs.

     In Regard you are like to have the most money, I have here En=
-closed sent an Invoice forgoods for the provision of Esqr. Wormeleys
family, and Desire you to send them in, in some good Shipp bound
for this River Upon the proper Accot. & risque of that Estate Marking them
wth. the Tobaccoe Mark, & Consigning them to me as One of the Exrs. as
Aforesaid, Andso I Desire you'll lett your Accot. Run. have, Not Else upon
this Subject at present butRes[pec]t[s]


Your humble Servant.

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Christ Church Parish, Lancaster County, Processioners' Returns, 1711-1783,and Wormeley Estate Papers, 1701-1710, 1716, Acc. 30126, Archives Research Services, Library of Virginia, Richmond, 142.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] John Burford commanded the Elizabeth and Mary, a London ship of 20 men and 10guns, built at Ipswich in 1697, and of 250 tons. She was owned by Goodwin and Mathews, SamuelLandford, Nicholas Goodwin, William Holditch, and Job Mathews. This ship sank during a voyage toVirginia in the winter of 1701-1702; John Burford later commanded the Mansfield by which Carter senttobacco to London. (1700 Convoy List; Navalofficer's return for Rappahannock River, CO5/1441, ff. 13)

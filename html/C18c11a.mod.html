Summary


March 11, 1718
Letter from Robert Carter to the Governor (AlexanderSpotswood) March 11, 1718, that his injuries received in a fall froma horse will prevent him from attending the yearly meeting of the"Governors of the College" (William and Mary) to choose a new rector,and that the trustees should "think it proper to choose anotherRector in my place." He adds that with the coming of warm weather,and with the assistance of "my friends coaches" he hopes to attendthe General Court.

     Robert Carter to Alexander Spotswood, March 11, 1718,

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Minor-Blackford Papers, James Monroe Law Office and Museum, Fredericksburg,Virginia. These texts are all nineteenth-century copies. Apparentlythere was in existence a letter book of Robert Carter's -- nowlost -- from which the unknown copiest recorded these texts. Asthey are the only texts, the punctuation and "corrections"obviously supplied by the copyist have been retained.

The return address, county, and colony have been added for clarity to this unheaded letter.


[1] The nineteenth-century copyist mistakenly added"Wm. Gooch" to the heading of a letter obviously entered in the original letter book asaddressed only to the governor, then Spotswood, not Gooch.

[2] The trustees of the College of William and Maryelected a Rector from among their own number. RC first became avisitor (trustee) of the College about 1716, and held the post of Rector formany years. As the College records for this period are not extant, itis impossible to determine Carter's periods of service asRector.

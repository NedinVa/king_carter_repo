Letter from Robert Carter to Colonel [Mann] Page, February 11, 1729

     Robert Carter writes to his son-in-law Colonel [Mann] Page, February 11, 1729, to turn over to him the management of the replacement as naval officer of the Rappahannock of Carter's son Robert with his son Charles.


Letter from Robert Carter to Colonel [Mann] Page, February 11, 1729

-1-

Corotomn, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

Febry: 11th: 1728/9

Colo: Page

Honble: Sir

     This accompanys my Son Charles who waits on you
in order to the Accomplishment of his great design he brings a letter
to you from his Brother and Also his Commission to be entirely un
der your direction in what as to the method you shall think it Advisable
to make use of them in I have also written to the Governor in an Open
letter for your perusal & either to be delivered or not as you shall
think most expedient you know what Vigilant and pressing ene
mies you we have to deal with their letters have flown about not only
through Virginia but Maryland Also I cannot think any great difficul
ties lie in the way If the governor will not come in to the immediate con:
ferring the place on my son Charles I shall think he waits for some
direction out of England and rather than lose the place altogether
my Son Robert had better hold it under the difficulties he now has
it than not at All By the heaviness of my Politics I have let
these dextrous gentlemen get the start of me having not written a
line to My Lord Orkney or any body Else since I came from Town
nor indeed have I had any Opportunity certain enough in my Opinion
to Make use of I shall entirely submit the management of this
affair to your prudcence & vigor. & Conduct & Am

Summary



Letter from Robert Carter to John Stark, August 11, 1729

     Robert Carter writes to Glasgow merchant John Stark, August 11, 1729, to report that extremely rainy weather has reduced the size of the crops by as much as one half both in Virginia and Maryland. He also notes a shipment of tobacco from the Burwell estate, a bill of exchange, and orders stockings and snuff.


Letter from Robert Carter to John Stark, August 11, 1729

-1-

Rappa[hannock, Lancaster County, Virginia]

Augst. 11th. 1729

Mr. John Stark

Sir.

     My last went by Capt. Stevenson and
brought you a bill of Lading for 8 hogsheads of Tobacco
We have had prodigeous rains for two months together that
hath very much destroyed our Crops the low lands Everywhere
are drowned and the high lands the tobacco washd so thin and
Carryed So hastily to the house [s] that our Crops must be very
Short we have intelligence from most parts of the Coun
try the rains have been general and we hear Maryland is
in the same Condition some Compute we shall not make ab
ove half the Tobacco we did last year I cannot think we
shall reach to two thirds I hope this will have a good Effect

-2-


upon the Tobo thats now gone home you have been very
dilatory in your Sales for some years past I beleive it will
be prudence not to be in too much hast in selling this year
I have sent you four hogsheads of Tobacco out of James River
belonging to Mr. Burwells Estate by Capt Roger which yo.
are to Accot for Accordingly Herein I send you a bill of
Excha: of my Son Robert for £5"0"0 which I desire
Credit for

     I sent an Invoice for some goods to you by Capt.
Bowman besides which I now desire you to send me twelve
doz of your largest pladd Stockins Such as you Sent me
last year they answer very well as Also three doz of yr:
Coarse large Worsted Stockins I am told you have
them from Aberdeen am in hopes your market now will
in some measure make up for the poor Accot. That you sent me last I am


Sir
yr very humble Servt:

I send yo herein ano: bill Mat Zuel on Miller Sterling & Company for
[. . .] And I draw upon yo payable to Mr [illegible][ . . . ] for £6
which I desire you to answer at time I desire you to send me
6 pints of yr best toasted snuff

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter Letter Book, 1727 April 13-1728 July 23, Carter Family Papers, Virginia Historical Society, Richmond.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity to the heading on the draft.

[1] John Stark was a merchant, probably the one to whom Carter referred when he wrote to Micajah Perry on 1723 July 4 that he had drawn an order on "Mr. Stark of Glasgow." On that same day, he wrote to a John Stark, referring to Captain Bowman.

[2] The Content was a Liverpool ship owned by John Pemberton and commanded by various masters including captains Stephenson (1721and later) , Fowler (1723), and Morton (1727). (Wright. Letters of Robert Carter. . . . pp. 92, 93, 102; and Carter to Pemberton, 1721 February 14, and 1727 June 28. )

[3] Captain Samuel Bowman commanded the Lucia. Carter mentioned this vessel in his diary in June 1724, and again on 1726 March 4 when he wrote that she "came in had 20 Weeks Passage."

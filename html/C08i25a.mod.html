September 25, 1708>
Letter from Robert Carter to Thomas Corbin, 1708 September25

     Robert Carter writes to London merchant Thomas Corbin, September25, 1708, that he no longer has any connection with the affairs ofthe estate of Ralph Wormeley because they now manage their ownproperties and he will no longer write to Corbin about affairs of theestate.


Robert Carter to Thomas Corbin, [September25, 1708]

-1-

[Rappahannock, Lancaster County,Virginia]
[September 25, 1708]
Mr Tho Corbin

     I have done Concerning my Self with YoungWormeleys affairs,their Estate is e [ntire]
ly under their own Govermt. & indeed it is time they addict themselves to business & so I take
my leave of it & You too from any further writing to You on their Accot: remaining

Yor affectionate Countryman &
humble Servant

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Christ ChurchParish, Lancaster County, Processioners' Returns, 1711-1783, andWormeley Estate Papers, 1701-1710, 1716, Acc. 30126, ArchivesResearch Services, Library of Virginia, Richmond, 179. Extractprinted William and Mary Quarterly , 1st. ser.,17(1909): 264.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of"Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than"Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especiallyto merchants abroad. The return addres, county, and colony have beenadded to this unheaded letter.


[1] This letter precedes another of this date in theletter book on the same theme.

[2] Carter often referred to in theiryouth as his "Cozns." Ralph Wormeley (ca.1681-1714), Ralph Wormeley's (d.1701) oldest son; and John Wormeley(1689-1727) because their parents were his brother-and sister-in-law. He was one of the boys' trustees under their father'swill.

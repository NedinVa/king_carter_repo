NOTES



Source copy consulted: Carter Family Papers, 1667-1862, Record Series Number: 01/Mss. 39.1 C24, Special Collections Research Center Earl Gregg Swem Library, College of William and Mary The sheet bearig this document has been damaged on both the right and left sides and portions of the text lost. The document, in the hand of an unidentified clerk, bears the signature and seal of Edmund Jenings as well as the signature of the clerk of the James City County court clerk, William Robertson, who has entered the authentication at the end of the document. The present editor has been greatly assisted in the transcription of this document by an earlier "transcription prepared by G. H. Reese and M. G. McGregor of Colonial Williamsburg's Research Department."


[1] A messuage is "the portion of land intended to be occupied, or actually occupied, as a site for a dwelling-house and its appurtenances." ( Oxford English Dictionary )

[2] "In 1706 Mrs. Mary Whaley established, in honor of her little son Matthew, a school in Bruton parish, York county, near Williamsburg, above York-Hampton parish, which she called Matteys School. She gave to the school ten acres of land. . . . In 1741-42, Mrs. Whaley, dying in England, left to the school a legacy of fifty pounds and the residue of her estate after payment of legacies. This particular sum, however, was not realized till a hundred years later." ( Lyon Gardiner Tyler, "Education in Colonial Virginia: Part III. Free Schools." William and Mary Quarterly. 6(October 1897): 70-85. )

[3] Teste is a Latin word used in legal documents "in the authenticating clause of a wrote . . . before [or after] the name of a person cited as witness or authority." ( Oxford English Dictionary )

Letter from Robert Carter to William Dawkins, July 30, 1731

     Robert Carter writes to William Dawkins, July 30, 1731, concerning debts due to Dawkins from Isaac Lee's estate.


Letter from Robert Carter to William Dawkins, July 30, 1731

-1-

Rappa [hannock, Lancaster County, Virginia]

July 30th: 1731

Mr: Wm: Dawkins

Sr

     We are now upon your Account against Isaac Lees Estate & your
Account Currt: with your letters Relating to Hancock Lees [illegible] Orphans the
State of which affair stands thus after Robert Carters Debts paid
you owe to that Estate 8£:11 Shillings :2 pence this Balance we ordered you to
carry to Isaac Lees Account We Sent you last year 6 hogsheads of stemmed
Straight laid Tobacco the produce of this also we ordered to be carried
to [illegible] that Account You give Credit [illegible] for payments made [illegible] upon our
orders amounting to 25:10:10 We now send you 1 hogshead more
of Stemmed Tobacco in the Bailey & a bill of exchange of John Lees for
6:1:0 Isaac Lees Debt is 94:0:6 when all these matters are
adjusted if the Tobacco Sells but tolerably suitably to other mens Sales of Such tobacco We cant but reckon
your Debt will be near accommodated, & Dos not Deserve the
hard expression you use (that it will be long a Discharging
at this rate) If you will hasten the Account of Sales, those
concerned promise to pay off what may remain due to you
& they Seem eager to do it, that the Brothers & Sisters
may divide what is due to them may make a Division of what will be left

     As to your [illegible] other Debts we have done you all the Service in
our power there is not above 3 or 4 [illegible] but what their Debts
have been demanded by Some of us excepting Such that we
never have nor can hear of. We have Enclosed Sent you a Copy
of your list with our Observations on those what we can do
towards [illegible] recovering of those of all that we have any hopes of you may depend on; You
have herein a bill of lading & a first bill of Exchange
for the Tobacco & mony already mentioned We are Sir


Yr. most humble Servants

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Letter book, 1731 July 9-1732 July 13 , Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] Isaac Lee (1700-1727) was a son of Hancock Lee and his second wife, Sarah Allerton. Isaac was a resident of Westmoreland County but died in England. (Descendants of John Lee , http://homepages.rootsweb.com/~kenzie/GenLEE.htm, 5/11/04)

[2] Hancock Lee (1653-1709) was an intimate friend of Robert Carter who was named in Lee's will as "a good friend," and appointed one of the trustees of his children. (Lee. Lee Chronicle . . . pp. 33-35.)

[3] The Bailey was a London ship owned by William Dawkins and commanded at various times by Adam Graves (1725-1730) and by Thomas Dove (1731-1732). She was a vessel of some 250 tons and carried 15-17 crew members. ( Survey report 6801 summarizing Adm. 68/195, 156v, and other data in Adm. 68/194 and /196, found in the microfilms of the Virginia Colonial Records Project, Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia; A letter of Carter's executors to Dawkins 1738 May 10 refers to "your ship Bailey.")

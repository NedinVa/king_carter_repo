Summary



Letter from Robert Carter to Edward Randolph, August 27, 1728

     Robert Carter writes to London merchant Edward Randolph, August 27, 1728, demanding payment of the proceeds of the sale of tobacco sent 2 or 3 years earlier by Robert Bolling to Randolph apparently in payment of a debt to Carter.


Letter from Robert Carter to Edward Randolph, August 27, 1728

-1 -

Rappahannock, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

Augst: the 27th: 1728

Mr. Edwd. Randolph

Sir --

     Colonel Robert Bolling of James River two
or three years ago Sent you 67 hogsheads of Tobbo: the Effects of which was
to be paid to my Order This Spring in the time of our last Assembly held in
March last he acquainted me he had an Accot. of the Sales of this Tobbo. from you
amounting to about Two Hundred and thirty one pounds
& telling me he had your Letter wherein you promised to be Account=
able for this money to me or my order I have already writ to Micajh.
Perry Esqr. to demand and receive it of you, who gives me no Accot.
hitherto about it have therefore thought it proper to Send to him
this order upon you desireing you will cause the Paymt. thereof to be
made to him on his order upon my Accot and he upon rect. will give you a discharge
from



Yor. humble Servt.

To
Mr: Edwd: Randolph
Mercht. in London
per Capt: Willcox

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter Letter Book, 1727 April 13-1728 July 23, Carter Family Papers, Virginia Historical Society, Richmond.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity to the heading on the draft.

[1] Little has been found about Randolph other than the fact that he may have come to live in Virginia as a man of that name was cited in York County records in 1740, "Edward Randolph of Loudoun, mercht, now residing in Virginia, devisee under the will of Graves Pack. . . ." ( William and Mary Quarterly. 3[Apr., 1895]269. )

[2] Robert Bolling (1682-1749) of Prince George County. ( "Bolling Family," http://homepages.rootsweb.com/~marshall/esmd19.htm, November 5, 2004. )

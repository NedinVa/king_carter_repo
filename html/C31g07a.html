Letter from Robert Carter to Peter How & Captain Richard Kelsick, July 7, 1731

     Robert Carter writes to Whitehaven merchants Peter How & Captain Richard Kelsick, July 7, 1731, concerning sales of tobacco he shipped on the Mazareen.


Letter from Robert Carter to Peter How & Captain Richard Kelsick, July 7, 1731

-1 -

Rappa[hannock, Lancaster County, Virginia]

July 7. 1731

Mr Peter How &Capt}
Richard Kelsick

Gentlemen

     I having the Opportunity of discoursing
CaptKelsick and understanding from him that you had sold
some of my 30 hogsheads of tobo sent yo in the Mazareen last year
when he came away I have nothing to add to that matter in that matter only to
wish you may meet with a good Market for them

     Herein you have a bill of Loading for 20:
hogsheads of tobo more in the same Ship consign'd to yo for sale
Resting in hopes that both this and the last Parcel may return a
living price I am,


Gentlemen,
Your very humble servant,

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Letter book, 1728 Aug.-1731 July , Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] Peter How was a merchant of Whitehaven on the Irish sea in northwest England, who, with Richard Kelsick, traded with the Fredericksburg, Virginia, area from the early 18th century. "He had interests in coal and iron-ore mines and set up an iron-working forge at Low Mill in 1750. Debts incurred by the forge and the decline in the tobacco trade may have contributed to his bankruptcy in 1763. " (" Fredericksburg and Whitehaven Connection to English port a forgotten chapter in area's Colonial history Living-history program," http://fredericksburg.com/News/FLS/2005/072005/07022005/111483/palm,, 2/22/2006; and Notes on a portrait of "Mrs Peter How and her Two Children, Peter and Christian," http://www.artfund.org/acq/artworkDetail4_5.asp?appref=2247, 2/22/2006. )

[2] Carter noted in his diary 1727 January 19 the arrival of the Mazareen at his house and a visit from her captain, Richard Kelsick, four days earlier.

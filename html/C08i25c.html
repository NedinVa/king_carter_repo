September 25, 1708
Letter from Robert Carter to Francis Lee, 1708 September25

     Robert Carter writes to London merchant Francis Lee, September25, 1708, that his last letter went by the Carter, and carried a billof lading for 18 hogsheads of tobacco. He thanks Lee for sending thecopies that he had requested, and notes that he now has nothing to dowith the affairs of the Wormeley estate and Lee will not hear fromhim again about them.


Robert Carter to Francis Lee, September25, 1708

-1 -

Rappa [hannock, LancasterCounty, Virginia]
Septemr 25th. 1708

Mr Francis Lee

     My last of 25 of May went by the Carter, brot as I hope bill of Ladin[g for 18]
hogsheads of Tobbaccosince I have Yors: by the Fleet wth. the Copys I desired f[or which]
mythanks. The Young Wormeleys have their Estate under their own [direction]
suppose by the directions of their mother, & no doubt You are among thei[rparticular?]
Friends, tis time for them to be acquainted with business & I'm clear out [This will]
be the last letter on their Accot.I remain


Yor Affect[iona] t[e ]. Coun[tryman &]
humble Servt.

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Christ ChurchParish, Lancaster County, Processioners' Returns, 1711-1783, andWormeley Estate Papers, 1701-1710, 1716, Acc. 30126, ArchivesResearch Services, Library of Virginia, Richmond, 179.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of"Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than"Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especiallyto merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added forclarity.

[1] Carter often referred to in theiryouth as his "Cozns." Ralph Wormeley (ca. 1681-1714), Ralph Wormeley's (d.1701) oldest son; and John Wormeley(1689-1727) because their parents were his brother-and sister-in-law. He was one of the boys' trustees under their father'swill.

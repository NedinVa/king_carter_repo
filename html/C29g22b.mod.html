Summary



Letter from Robert Carter to John Zuil, July 22, 1729

     Robert Carter writes to Liverpool merchant John Zuil, July 22, 1729, to complain that he has yet to receive an account of the sales of his last shipment of tobacco, and to enclose a bill of lading (not present) for more. He also orders clothes.


Letter from Robert Carter to John Zuil, July 22, 1729

-1 -

Rappa [hannock, Lancaster County, Virginia]

July 22d. 1729

Mr John Zuil

Sir

     The Tobacco I sent you by Captain Stevenson
when he was here before has met with a very tedious dispose
I have your letter Acquainting me how you had dispersed it but
what it will yield me I am yet a Stranger to your being an owner
in the Caledon hath prevailed with me to make a further small
Trial of your management herein is a bill of Lading for 6 hogsheads
Consigned you upon my Accot & risk I wish they may answer so
well to Encourage a further correspondence with you I am


Sir
your very Humble Servant

I desire you to send
me in a Handsome Morning
Gown & a pair of Leg boots or spattered asshes with Large broad
buckles and handsome Spurs and leathers to them

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Letter book, 1728 August-1731 July, Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] John Zuil was a merchant and was probably the ship's captain that Carter mentioned in his diary 1722 August 1, "Zuil Saild Gave me a Bottle Snuff." Carter recorded a diary note about him the following year as well: 1723 December 30 "mr Zuil & man came back" [from church]. In what British city Zuil lived is not clear, but it may have been Liverpool because city directories of 1767-1773 list a John Zuil as a merchant, first in Cable Street, and later, in King Street. This probably would have been a son of the man Carter knew, given the shorter lives at this period. ( "Yuil Family Newsletters," Issue #24 Fall. 1998http://www.geocities.com/yulefamily/newsletters/yule24.htm, September 30, 2004 )

[2] The Content was a Liverpool ship owned by John Pemberton and commanded by various masters including captains Stephenson (1721 and later) , Fowler (1723), and Morton (1727). (Wright. Letters of Robert Carter. . . . pp. 92, 93, 102 ; and Carter to Pemberton, 1721 February 14, and 1727 June 28. )

[3] The clerk should have written "spatterdashes," a type of knee-high leggings worn over other garments to protect them from splashes and mud.

Letter from Robert Carter to John Sterling and John Zuil, June 3, 1727

     Robert Carter writes to John Sterling and John Zuil, June 3, 1727, a newly-formed partnership, to send them a bill of lading (not present) for tobacco that he is shipping to them on board Captain Stephenson's ship. He notes that 3 hogsheads are sweet scented tobacco, and promises more business to the firm if they sell his tobacco at a good price.


Letter from Robert Carter to John Sterling and John Zuil, June 3, 1727

-1-

Rappahannock, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

June the. 3d1727

Messrs: John Sterling & John Zuil

Gent --

     Capt: Stephenson acquainting me wth.
your partnership makes me address to you both my Freindship
is only with Mr. Zuil upon whose desire I now Send you 14 hhds
of Tobbo: I will Say nothing in Commendation of it leaving it to
Speak for it Self only that the three hhds numbred with Letters
are Sweet Sented Tobbo: and the whole Crop of a Small plantation
of mine a Bill of Lading for this Tobbo: you have here inclosd
I promised Capt: Stephenson 30 hhds: if he Stayd long Enough
for my geting it home and making it Shipable As he will tell you
but he hath bin so Expeditious in his business that I could not
be ready with any more for him and it was not worth his while
to wait my Time for the rest, If you please me in the Dispose of this
you will Entitle to your Selves to further business from


Gentlemen
Yor: humble Servt:

per Capt: Stephenson

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter letter book, 1727 May-1728 July, Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] John Zuil probably was the ship's captain that Carter noted in his diary as having sailed 1722 August 1. This is the only letter to this new firm that is currently (6/4/2003) extant, and no other information about these men has been located.

[2] A Captain Stephenson was master of the Content in 1721, but a ship of that name was commanded by Captain Morton in 1727. (Wright. Letters of Robert Carter. . . . pp. 92, 93, 102; and Carter to Pemberton, 1721 February 14, and 1727 June 28.)

Summary



Letter from Robert Carter to Captain John Hyde & Company, May 17, 1727,

     Robert Carter writes to Captain John Hyde & Company, May 17, 1727, reporting the arrival of letters and goods from the London merchant, and complaining that "your sales of my Tobbo: were the lowest of any that I had met." He gives details of the stay of the company's ship, the Providence, in the colony, sends a bill of lading for 20 hogsheads of tobacco on board the ship, and reports a bill of exchange that he has drawn on Captain Woodward.


Letter from Robert Carter to Captain John Hyde & Company, May 17, 1727

-1 -

Rappa[hannock, Lancaster County, Virginia]

May 17th. 1727.

Capt: John Hyde & Company

Gent: --

     I have rec'd your Several Letters & goods
your sales of my Tobbo: were the lowest of any that I had met
Indeed it hath bin my Misfortune all along to have but low
Sales from you what ever hath bin the reason of it Mr. Levitt is a Safe man but he always gives
the lowest prices You give me hopes of your doing better hereaf
ter. Your ship hath made a long Stay here Your master is to accot:
for it My Tobbo: hath bin ready for him ever Since he came in
I only waited till his ground Tire was laid and then he had it,

     herein Send you a bill of Lading for 20 hhds of
my own Crop Tobbo: made at my home plantations prized
under my Eye Sweet Scented Tobbo: I make no other and I
promise my Self is very good I hope will merit an Inland
Sale and give me the Advantage of the Discounts,

     I have now drawn upon you a bill of Exche:
payable to Capt: Woodward for £34:15:-which I desire you
to answer on my Accot: he wanted two hhds. to full him up
I let him have them I ask him what freight he would want
of me His answer was 30 would do He might have had more
if he had wanted it I heartily wish your Ship Safe to you & am,
Gent.
Yor: most humble Servt.




per Woodward

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter letter book, 1727 May-1728 July, Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] Mr. Levitt has not been identified.

[2] Carter probably means the ship's mooring for her time in Virginia.

[3] Thomas Woodward commanded the Providence, a ship owned by Captain John Hyde & Company, during a number of voyages to the colony, 1723-1727. ( Adm. 68/194 and 195, Virginia Colonial Records Project, Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia, and Carter's letter to the firm, 1723 September 17.)

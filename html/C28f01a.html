Summary



Letter from Robert Carter to John Pemberton and Company, June 1,1728

     Robert Carter writes to Liverpool mechant John Pemberton and Company, June 1,1728, to cover bills of lading for tobacco, some of which Carter has received in partial payment from a Purcell for his debt to Carter.


Letter from Robert Carter to John Pemberton and Company, June 1,1728

-1-

Rappahannock, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

June the 1st. 1728

Mr John Pemberton & Company
Ownrs. of John & Betty


     This Accompanys Capt: Denton and
brings you a bill of Lading for 15 hogsheads of Tobbo: markt PD
on board the John and Betty Also for 3 hogsheads markt J~B in Do:
Ship and a bill of Lading for 1 hogshead more in the Rappahannock Capt.
Brackhill, You have Accots: Sent you of this Concern as far as
it is gon and is Compleated to Some Small Debts Excepting [illegible] Purcells
Debt. part of which my Agreemt. was to be paid next year.
The money ballance due to you I make to be £16:10:9 for Paymt.

-2-


thereof I give directions to Mr. Pemberton, 2 prizd hogsheads more
a further payment of Purcells debt I intend to Ship on the
Carter for London no prospect offering to Send it [to] Yor. own port I am


Gent --
Yor. very humble Servt.

per Denton
Copy per Trice

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Robert Carter Letter Book, 1727 April 13-1728 July 23, Carter Family Papers, Virginia Historical Society, Richmond.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity to the heading on the draft.

[1] The John & Betty was a Liverpool ship owned by merchant John Pemberton; she often carryied slaves into the colony. In 1726 the captain was John Gale, and in the next year, she was commanded by a Captain William Denton. The ship would be lost in 1729. (Wright. Letters of Robert Carter. . . . p. 18, n. 23; Carter to Pemberton, 1730 April 15; and Carter to William Dawkins, June 28, July 26, and August 22, 1727, for Denton's first name.)

[2] There were several vessels with the name Rappahannock. One was based in Liverpool and was commanded by a Captain Francis Etheridge in 1725; she was a 90 ton ship with 13 men. Another of which Charles Whate was captain was of 60 tons with 11 men in 1726 and 1727. Carter's letters mention a Captain Brackhill in 1728, and Captain Loxum in 1733. ( Adm. 68/194, ff.30, found in the microfilms of the Virginia Colonial Records Project, Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.)

[3] The Pursell to whom Carter refers probably was Robert Pursell from whom he acquired the Brick House property in Richmond County. Carter's 1732 inventory entry for this property mentions "things formerly belonging to Robert Purtell" (Pursell). (Miller. Place-Names . . . . p. 15; and "Carter Papers: An Inventory. . . .")

[4] The 140 ton Welcome was owned by London merchant James Bradley to whom Carter would write about her on 1727 May 17. John Trice (Frice) was her captain, 1723-1728. ( Survey Report 6801 summarizing Adm 68/195, 154r, found in the microfilms of the Virginia Colonial Records Project, Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.)

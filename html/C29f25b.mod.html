Summary



Letter from Robert Carter to James Bradley, June 25, 1729

     Robert Carter writes to London merchant James Bradley, June 25, 1729, to cover a bill of lading (not present) for tobacco.


Letter from Robert Carter to James Bradley, June 25, 1729

-1 -

Rappahannock, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

June the 25th. 1729

Mr. James Bradly

Sir --

     This is Just a line to Cover a bill of Lading for 10 hogsheads
of Tobacco Consigned you in the Forward Captain Loney. It was made at my home
Plantations and I hope will prove good, I shall only wish it safe to you
and Conclude


Sir --
Your . most humble Servant

per Trice

NOTES



Source copy consulted: Letter book, 1728 August-1731 July, Robert Carter Papers (acc. no. 3807), Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of "Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than "Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especially to merchants abroad. The county and colony have been added for clarity.

[1] James Bradley was a London merchant with whom Carter dealt from at least 1723 until his death. As noted in his letter to Bradley of 1727 May 17, Bradley owned the Welcome, but little information about Bradley has been located.

[2] The Forward was a London ship of 150-200 tons commanded in 1728-29 by William Loney, and in 1731 by George Buckridge. ( Survey Report 6800 summarizing Adm. 68/194 and Survey Report 6801 summarizing Adm 68/195, Virginia Colonial Records Project, Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library, University of Virginia. )

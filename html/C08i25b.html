Letter from Robert Carter to John Goodwin, 1708 September25

     Robert Carter writes to London merchant John Goodwin, September25, 1708, that the sons of Ralph Wormeley have assumed management oftheir own affairs, and thus, he will not write again about theestate. He adds that he has drawn a bill of exchange on Goodwin toDoctor John Lomax to pay a debt of the estate.


Robert Carter to John Goodwin, September25, 1708

-1 -

[Rappahannock, LancasterCounty, Virginia
[September 25,1708]
Mr. Jno Goodwin

     The Young Wormeleys are now Supravisors [sic] of their own Estate Iha [ve told]
them nor will You further hear from me on their Accot only have now [to tell]
You I have drawn on Youfor 4£ 10p to Doctor

[John] Lomax being a debt due from [the estate]
& You are to pay it accordingly Charging it to the Debit thereof


I am
Yor humble Servt

NOTES



Source copy consulted: ChristChurch Parish, Lancaster County, Processioners' Returns, 1711-1783,and Wormeley Estate Papers, 1701-1710, 1716, Acc. 30126,Archives Research Services, Library of Virginia, Richmond, 179.

This letter precedes another of this date in the letter bookon the same theme.

Robert Carter generally used a return address of"Rappahannock" for the river on which he lived rather than"Corotoman," the name of his home, on his correspondence, especiallyto merchants abroad. The return address, county, and state have beenadded for clarity to this unheaded letter.

[1] Carter often referred to in theiryouth as his "Cozns." Ralph Wormeley (ca. 1681-1714), Ralph Wormeley's (d.1701) oldest son; and John Wormeley(1689-1727) because their parents were his brother-and sister-in-law. He was one of the boys' trustees under their father'swill.

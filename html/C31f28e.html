Letter from Robert Carter to John Randolph, June 28, 1731

     Robert Carter writes to attorney John Randolph, June 28, 1731, to send him bills of exchange for a purchase that he and his son, Secretary John Carter, wish to have Randolph arrange.


Letter from Robert Carter to John Randolph, June 28, 1731

-1 -

Corotoman, [Lancaster County, Virginia]

28 June 1731

To John Randolph Esqr

Sir

     According to your Desire I now send you three Sets of
bills of Excha, [illegible] For on my own Accot for £56.7.6 on Mr Pemberton
of Leverpool, the other two sets for one hundred & fifty three pounds
& Sixpence on Accot of Colo Page's Estate on Mr Perry; to these Sets the
Secretary's Hand must be had & I absolutely depend upon your
Word for it. Mine being an Outport Bill may perhaps not be
so very agreeable. If it will be the least Detriment or Disappoint=
ment to you, return thm by my Son & I will send you London Bills
in the Room of them. I had some Apprehensions these Bills upon
yr accot of Colonel Page were to be delayed [illegible] till the going out of some of the
Latter Ships; how Mr Secretary will relish giving thm so soon I can't say
you know what a ticklish affair this is, but I can't think a little time
will make any Difference with our Creditor, altho' in our Advices we
told him our Bills would not come upon him till the latter Ships
I believe in two or three months I may have Occasion for a --
pritty large Sum of Cash it may be a hundred pounds or more; how your Stock may hold out you will please to
                                                                                            advise

-2 -


advise me I shall look out to no other; I have several in my View tht
will willingly embrace my Custom, but I shall not look further
if you will supply me. I am in no doubt you have taken proper Care
in the Security of my Purchase. I heartily wish you the Enjoymt of your Intire
Health & am


Sr
Your most humble Servant

I send a Lr of advice with
my Bills on Pemberton If you
return thm please to send that back to me also.
